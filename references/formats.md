# Format Guides

Norms, slop-in-the-wild, and good examples per format. The voice rules in SKILL.md apply everywhere; this file covers what changes between formats.

## Blog post / newsletter

**Norms**: As short as the idea allows — a 500-word post that says one thing well beats 1,200 words of coverage. Few or no headers under 800 words. Prose, not listicle. First person is normal. Opinions are the point.

**Slop shape**: SEO-intro restating the title, header-per-section each with 2–3 bullets, a "Final Thoughts" section, moral ending. If the post could be regenerated from its own title, it's slop.

**Do instead**:
- Open mid-story or with the sharpest claim. No warm-up paragraph.
- Structure as one continuous argument or story. A digression is fine; label it honestly ("Side note —").
- Include the numbers and names that make it yours: dates, prices, tools, screenshots-worth-of-detail — all from the user's real input, never invented (see the prove-it test in SKILL.md). If the user gave you three specifics, a post built honestly on three beats one padded with ten fakes.
- End early, on a specific. Delete any paragraph that begins "Ultimately" or "In the end".

**Ending example** (post about switching note apps):
- ❌ "Ultimately, the best tool is the one that gets out of your way. Whatever system you choose, make sure it works for you."
- ✅ "It's been four months. The only thing I miss is the /table command, and I don't miss it much."

## LinkedIn post

**Norms**: 60–150 words. Short lines, some whitespace is native to the platform. First person. One concrete story or observation per post.

**Slop shape**: Hook line + "Let me explain." + numbered revelations + engagement bait ("Agree?", "Thoughts?", "Repost if this resonated"). Vulnerability theater ("I got rejected 47 times. Then everything changed."). Emoji bullets. "Humbled and honored to announce".

**Do instead**:
- Announce things like you'd tell a friend who half-cares: plainly, with one detail that makes it real.
- Skip "excited/thrilled/humbled" — say what's actually happening and maybe one true feeling ("bit nervous, mostly ready").
- No call-to-action unless you genuinely want the thing.

**Example** (new job):
- ❌ "🎉 Thrilled to announce that I'm embarking on a new journey as Marketing Associate at Hop Skip Media! Huge thanks to everyone who supported me along the way. Here's to new beginnings! 🚀"
- ✅ "Starting at Hop Skip Media on Monday as a marketing associate. I've been freelancing for their team since March, so mostly this means I stop invoicing them and get a desk. Looking forward to it."

## X / Twitter

**Norms**: One thought per post. Lowercase is fine if the user writes that way. Specifics or jokes; nothing in between survives.

**Slop shape**: "Hot take:" followed by consensus. Thread-bait ("🧵"). Fake profundity ("Most people won't get this."). The "X isn't Y. It's Z." format is endemic here — banned.

## Instagram caption

**Norms**: 1–4 short lines for most posts. Emojis allowed but ≤3, and never as bullets. Price, availability, location — the details people actually screenshot. Hashtags if the user uses them; a handful, at the end.

**Slop shape**: "✨ There's something magical about..." + "It's not just a plant — it's a lifestyle" + 12 emojis + 25 hashtags.

**Example** (rare plant restock):
- ❌ "✨🌿 Calling all plant lovers! Our stunning Thai Constellations have arrived to elevate your urban jungle! These beauties won't last long! 🌿✨"
- ✅ "Thai Constellation restock. 6 plants, ₹4,500 each, all with at least one new leaf coming in. DMs open from 10am, first come first served. Last time these went in a day."

## Cold email

**Norms**: Under 120 words. Subject line = plain and specific ("your site on mobile", not "Quick question 🚀" or "Unlocking growth for [Company]"). One observation, one offer, one easy next step. Sign off simply.

**Slop shape**: "I hope this email finds you well. My name is X and I'm reaching out because... We help businesses like yours leverage... I'd love to hop on a quick call."

**Do instead**:
- Lead with the specific thing you noticed about *them* — real, checkable, no flattery.
- Say what you do in one sentence with proof (a name they can look up).
- Ask a small question, not "a quick 15-minute call".

**Example**:
- ❌ "Dear Maria, I hope this message finds you well! I'm a passionate web developer dedicated to helping small businesses elevate their online presence..."
- ✅ "Hi Maria, your menu page took about 8 seconds to load on my phone (tested on 4G this morning). That's usually enough for people to give up and check Google Maps instead. I rebuild sites for small food businesses; did a bakery's shop page last year. Want me to send a 2-minute video of what I'd fix first? Alex"

## Work / everyday email

**Norms**: Answer in the first line. Context after, if needed. Length matches the ask — two lines is a complete email. Greetings match the relationship; "Hi Priya," is fine, "I hope this email finds you well" is banned.

**Slop shape**: Restating their question back, three paragraphs of context before the answer, "Please do not hesitate to reach out should you have any further questions."

## Cover letter

**Norms**: 250–350 words. Structure: why this specific role/company (one real reason, not "your prestigious organization") → 1–2 evidence stories with real details → a plain close. Contractions are fine. Sound like a person the reader might want to sit near.

**Slop shape**: "I am writing to express my keen interest... proven track record... results-driven team player... I am confident my skill set aligns perfectly... I would welcome the opportunity to discuss... Thank you for considering my application."

**Do instead**:
- Name the actual thing that drew you to them (a product, a campaign, a job-post line).
- Tell the achievement as a small story with friction: what was broken, what you did, what happened. Use only real numbers the user gave you. No numbers offered? Describe the change qualitatively — don't invent.
- Admit an edge honestly if useful ("I haven't run paid ads at this scale; I have run them at ₹40k/month").
- Close plainly: "I'd like to talk about this role. Here's my portfolio: [link]."

**Opening example**:
- ❌ "I am excited to apply for the Content Marketing Executive position at your esteemed organization."
- ✅ "I've spent two years making people care about houseplants on Instagram. A family shop, no ad budget, 800 to 6,000 followers. I'd like to do the harder version of that for Flipkart."
